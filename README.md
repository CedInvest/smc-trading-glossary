## Order Block (OB)

🇫🇷 **Order Block** — Bloc d'ordre.

**Definition.** The last consolidation candle (or cluster) before an impulsive directional move. A bullish OB is the last bearish candle before a strong bullish breakout; a bearish OB is the last bullish candle before a strong bearish breakdown. Order Blocks mark zones where institutional players accumulated orders and act as dynamic support/resistance on retest.

**Mnemonic.** "Last opposite-color candle before the impulse."

**Trading application.** Identify higher-timeframe trend → locate last unmitigated OB in trend direction → wait for price to retrace into the zone → confirm with a structure signal (rejection, FVG fill, divergence) → enter with stop just beyond the OB and take-profit at the next opposing liquidity zone. Validity typically degrades after 2-3 retests.

**Related.** [Mitigation Block](#mitigation-block), [Breaker Block](#breaker-block), [Fair Value Gap](#fair-value-gap-fvg)
**Read more.** [Investisseur 2.0 — Order Blocks crypto guide](https://investisseur2-0.com/en/resources/order-blocks-crypto.html)

## Fair Value Gap (FVG)

🇫🇷 **Fair Value Gap** — Imbalance, gap de valeur.

**Definition.** An unfilled price gap between the high of candle N-1 and the low of candle N+1 (bullish FVG), or between the low of candle N-1 and the high of candle N+1 (bearish FVG). Reveals a market inefficiency that price statistically tends to revisit and fill. Quick formation = signal of strong institutional momentum.

**Mnemonic.** "Three candles, no overlap between candle 1 and candle 3."

**Trading application.** FVGs act as both targets (price comes to fill them) and entries (limit orders placed inside the gap). Combine with an Order Block in the same area for high-probability confluence.

**Related.** [Order Block](#order-block-ob), [Liquidity Void](#liquidity-void)
**Read more.** [Investisseur 2.0 — Fair Value Gap explained](https://investisseur2-0.com/en/resources/fair-value-gap-fvg.html)

## Break of Structure (BOS)

🇫🇷 **Break of Structure** — Cassure de structure.

**Definition.** Price closes beyond the previous swing high (bullish BOS) or swing low (bearish BOS) in the direction of the prevailing trend. Confirms trend continuation.

**Mnemonic.** "Trend says yes."

**Trading application.** Use BOS to validate that an impulse move continues. After a BOS, look for retracement entries in the new range (Order Block or FVG inside the OTE zone).

**Related.** [Change of Character](#change-of-character-choch)

---

## Change of Character (CHoCH)

🇫🇷 **Change of Character** — Changement de caractère.

**Definition.** Price closes beyond a swing high or swing low against the prevailing trend. First structural signal of a possible reversal. CHoCH precedes BOS in the new direction.

**Mnemonic.** "Trend says wait."

**Trading application.** A CHoCH alone is not a buy/sell trigger — wait for a BOS in the new direction or a Mitigation Block formation to confirm the reversal.

**Related.** [Break of Structure](#break-of-structure-bos), [Mitigation Block](#mitigation-block)

---

## Mitigation Block

🇫🇷 **Mitigation Block** — Bloc de mitigation.

**Definition.** An Order Block that has already been visited and partially "mitigated" (some institutional orders filled) on a first test. On a second retest, residual unfilled orders activate, making it a high-probability S/R zone.

**Trading application.** A Mitigation Block on the second test is one of the highest-probability SMC setups when combined with structural BOS in the same direction.

**Related.** [Order Block](#order-block-ob), [Breaker Block](#breaker-block)

---

## Breaker Block

🇫🇷 **Breaker Block** — Bloc breaker.

**Definition.** An Order Block whose structure was broken (BOS in the opposite direction). The OB now flips role: a former bullish OB becomes resistance after a bearish BOS through it.

**Mnemonic.** "Broken OB, opposite role."

**Related.** [Order Block](#order-block-ob), [Mitigation Block](#mitigation-block)

---

## Inducement

🇫🇷 **Induction** (terme rare).

**Definition.** A small liquidity zone deliberately planted by market makers to entice retail traders into early entries before a larger sweep in the opposite direction.

**Trading application.** Identify potential inducements as obvious-looking levels (round numbers, recent swing highs/lows). Wait for the inducement to be swept before taking the real institutional setup.

---

## Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL)

🇫🇷 **Liquidité acheteuse** (BSL).

**Definition.** The cluster of stop-losses placed by short sellers and breakout-buy orders located above a swing high. Privileged target of bullish institutional manipulation.

**Trading application.** Anticipate that price will sweep BSL zones before reversing. Avoid placing stops directly at swing highs — use a buffer of 1-1.5× ATR.

**Related.** [Sell-Side Liquidity](#sell-side-liquidity-ssl), [Liquidity Sweep](#liquidity-sweep--stop-hunt)

---

## Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL)

🇫🇷 **Liquidité vendeuse** (SSL).

**Definition.** The cluster of stop-losses placed by long traders and breakout-sell orders located below a swing low. Target of bearish institutional manipulation.

**Related.** [Buy-Side Liquidity](#buy-side-liquidity-bsl)

---

## Liquidity Sweep / Stop Hunt

🇫🇷 **Sweep de liquidité** / **Chasse aux stops**.

**Definition.** A brief price move that hunts a liquidity zone (BSL or SSL) before reversing in the opposite direction. Typically precedes a high-momentum directional expansion.

**Mnemonic.** "Wick goes hunting, body goes the other way."

**Trading application.** A liquidity sweep followed by a CHoCH is a classic reversal setup. Enter on the first Order Block formed inside the sweep, with stop beyond the sweep wick.

**Related.** [Buy-Side Liquidity](#buy-side-liquidity-bsl), [Sell-Side Liquidity](#sell-side-liquidity-ssl)
**Read more.** [Investisseur 2.0 — Understanding institutional liquidity](https://investisseur2-0.com/en/resources/comprendre-liquidite.html)

## Equal Highs (EQH) and Equal Lows (EQL)

🇫🇷 **Sommets égaux / Creux égaux**.

**Definition.** Multiple highs (or lows) aligned at virtually the same price level, creating a concentrated liquidity pool. Statistically very high targeting probability by institutional players.

**Trading application.** EQH/EQL are always swept eventually. Use them as targets, not as resistance/support to trade against.

## Liquidity Void

🇫🇷 **Vide de liquidité**.

**Definition.** A large empty space on the chart with no significant trading activity, typically created by a violent breakout. Behaves like a magnet — price often returns to fill it before continuing the trend.

**Related.** [Fair Value Gap](#fair-value-gap-fvg)

## Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)

🇫🇷 **Entrée optimale** (concept ICT).

**Definition.** The Fibonacci retracement zone between 62% and 79% considered by ICT as the optimal entry zone in trend direction after a BOS. Corresponds to the "Discount" area in a bullish trend and "Premium" in bearish trend, ideally with confluence from an Order Block and FVG.

**Trading application.** Plot Fibonacci from the most recent swing low to swing high (or inverse). Look for OB + FVG inside the 0.62-0.79 zone for high-probability entries.

**Related.** [Premium / Discount](#premium--discount), [Order Block](#order-block-ob)

## Killzone

🇫🇷 **Killzone** (terme conservé).

**Definition.** Time windows of high institutional activity where SMC setups have the highest statistical probability. Three primary killzones (CET):
- **Asia Killzone** — 21h-3h. Accumulation phase, range-bound.
- **London Open** — 8h-11h. First major manipulation, often a stop hunt at the Asia range high or low.
- **New York Open** — 14h-17h. Highest volume of the day, strongest directional momentum.

**Trading application.** Trade SMC setups primarily during London and NY killzones. Asia session for analysis and OB identification, not aggressive entries.

## Premium / Discount

🇫🇷 **Zone Premium / Discount** (zones d'achat/vente Fibonacci).

**Definition.** Concept ICT dividing a range at the 50% Fibonacci level. Above 50% = Premium (smart money sells), below 50% = Discount (smart money buys). Central concept for identifying institutional entry zones.

**Trading application.** Only buy in Discount, only sell in Premium — never against this rule on your higher timeframe.

**Related.** [Optimal Trade Entry](#optimal-trade-entry-ote)

## Power of Three (PO3)

🇫🇷 **Pouvoir du Trois**.

**Definition.** ICT framework describing the typical institutional manipulation cycle in three phases: **Accumulation** (ranging), **Manipulation** (false breakout / stop hunt against the trend), then **Distribution / Expansion** (real directional move). Applies to daily, weekly and monthly cycles.

**Trading application.** Identify each phase on your higher timeframe to anticipate the manipulation move and trade the expansion. Often: London Open = manipulation, NY Open = expansion.

## Judas Swing

🇫🇷 **Swing de Judas**.

**Definition.** A false move at the open of London or NY session that traps retail traders before reversing in the true direction. Named after the betrayal of Judas — looks legit but stabs you in the back.

**Trading application.** Wait 30-60 minutes after London/NY open before entering. The Judas Swing usually completes within the first hour.

## Accumulation

🇫🇷 **Accumulation** (phase Wyckoff).

**Definition.** Wyckoff phase where smart money quietly absorbs supply at the lows, typically in a sideways range. Marked by Selling Climax (SC), Automatic Rally (AR), Secondary Test (ST), and Spring (final stop hunt below the range).

**Trading application.** Identify accumulation ranges on weekly/daily — these precede major bull moves.

**Related.** [Manipulation / Markup](#manipulation--markup), [Distribution](#distribution)

---

## Manipulation / Markup

🇫🇷 **Manipulation / Hausse**.

**Definition.** Wyckoff phase following accumulation. Smart money initiates the upward move while still triggering retail stops below the range (manipulation) before driving prices higher (markup).

---

## Distribution

🇫🇷 **Distribution**.

**Definition.** Wyckoff phase where smart money offloads positions to retail at the highs. Marked by Buying Climax (BC), Automatic Reaction (AR), Secondary Test (ST), and Upthrust After Distribution (UTAD).

**Related.** [Accumulation](#accumulation)

---

## Markdown

🇫🇷 **Markdown / Baisse**.

**Definition.** Wyckoff phase following distribution where smart money is short and price falls into the next accumulation zone.

## The 1% Rule

🇫🇷 **Règle du 1 %**.

**Definition.** Risk management rule stipulating that no single trade should put more than 1% of total capital at risk. With $10,000 capital, maximum loss per trade = $100.

**Mathematical justification.** With strict 1% per trade and a typical 50% win rate at 1:2 R/R, theoretical edge = +0.5% per trade. Drawdown of 10 consecutive losses = -10% (recoverable). Without this rule, a 5-loss streak at 5% per trade = -25% (psychologically devastating, statistically near-fatal).

**Related.** [Risk/Reward](#riskreward-rr), [ATR Position Sizing](#atr-position-sizing)
**Read more.** [Investisseur 2.0 — Position size calculator (1% rule)](https://investisseur2-0.com/en/resources/calculateur-risque-crypto.html)

## Risk/Reward (R/R)

🇫🇷 **Ratio risque/récompense**.

**Definition.** Ratio of potential gain to potential loss on a trade. Calculated as `(Take-Profit - Entry) / (Entry - Stop-Loss)` for a long trade.

**Trading application.** Never take a trade with R/R below 1:2. SMC institutional setups typically target 1:3 to 1:5.

---

## ATR Position Sizing

🇫🇷 **Sizing par ATR**.

**Definition.** Position sizing methodology that adjusts the trade size to the asset's volatility (Average True Range) to normalize risk across different instruments. Formula: `Position Size = (Capital × Risk%) / (ATR × Multiplier)`.

**Trading application.** Use ATR(14) on the trading timeframe. Multiplier of 1.5 to 2 for stop placement. This prevents under-sizing on low-volatility assets and over-sizing on highly volatile ones.

**Related.** [The 1% Rule](#the-1-rule)
**Read more.** [Investisseur 2.0 — ATR trading crypto](https://investisseur2-0.com/en/resources/atr-trading-crypto.html)

## Drawdown

🇫🇷 **Drawdown / Repli maximal**.

**Definition.** The peak-to-trough decline of an account or strategy, expressed as a percentage of peak equity.

**Investisseur 2.0 reference.** Drawdown <15% over a rolling 3-month window is acceptable. Beyond, a mandatory pause and process review is required.

**Read more.** [Investisseur 2.0 — Drawdown management](https://investisseur2-0.com/en/resources/drawdown-maximal-gestion-risque.html)
